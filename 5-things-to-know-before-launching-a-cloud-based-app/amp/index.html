5 Things to Know Before Launching a Cloud Based App

Rae Steinbach
5 months ago

Cloud based apps for business are growing increasingly popular. According to one survey, 80% of businesses are increasing their usage of cloud-managed services.

Developing cloud based apps, whether for your own organization or for your customers, is a very smart way to leverage the technology. For instance, cloud based apps can reduce the cost of many IT processes, resulting in major savings in the long run.

That said, there are some points you should keep in mind before launching this type of product. Proper development and implementation is key to successfully using a cloud based app for your business. That’s why, when working on such a project, it’s important to consider the following:

How the Organization Will Adopt the Cloud

Shifting to cloud based IT processes can be challenging if current methods are entrenched in the organization. One survey indicates that 32% of companies lack sufficient resources to easily make the switch.

Before attempting to transfer over to cloud based apps, you should consult with IT professionals to ensure you have the resources you need to seamlessly adopt the technology. Otherwise, you risk causing more harm than good internally while switching over.

The Infrastructure Necessary for Developing Cloud Based Apps

It’s also important to discuss infrastructure needs with both IT experts and app developers. Your organization’s current hardware and software infrastructure may not support the product. You’ll save yourself a lot of time and frustration if you make the necessary upgrades before trying to switch to cloud based apps for your business.

Private or Public?

Companies can reduce costs by following the public model of cloud based apps and computing, in which various organizations share resources. However, if you’re concerned about security, or if your organization needs access to a large amount of resources, you may be better off with a private model. There are also hybrid approaches that combine benefits of both.

Potential Future Growth

Flexibility is one of the main reasons cloud based apps for business are useful. As your organization’s needs grow, the app can grow along with you.

However, that means you may need to make upgrades to your infrastructure again in the future. Consider your expectations for future growth, and prepare accordingly in order to take full advantage of the flexibility a cloud based app offers.

Disaster & Data Recovery Best Practices

According to one report, three out of four companies receive failing grades when their disaster recovery plans are evaluated. You don’t want to be one of those organizations.

Consult with big data and cloud based technology professionals to make sure you understand disaster recovery best practices, especially as they pertain to cloud based apps. Although you never want to rely on a data recovery plan, it may be necessary at some point. Having a strong plan in place ensures that operations run smoothly if disaster ever strikes.

The benefits of developing a cloud based app for your business are significant. You just need to make sure you’ve taken necessary steps to fully reap these advantages. By planning ahead, you’ll be much better equipped to use this technology to its full potential.
