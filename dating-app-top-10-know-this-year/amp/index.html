The Top 10 Dating Apps to Know This Year

Rae Steinbach
4 days ago

Meeting people is hard and getting to know them on an intimate level is even harder. Thankfully, apps have made dating a lot easier. Being able to open a dating app and find singles in your area just  by checking your phone is simply a convenient way to meet someone.

That said, there is a reason more than just one dating application is popular: different apps offer unique features and benefits. To better understand how they differ, check out our dating app top 10 list below.

Tinder App Emphasizes Ease of Use in Mobile Dating

Tinder oftens tops the list of dating app rankings thanks to its ease-of-use. Allowing users to showcase up to six pictures while also limiting bios to 500 characters, In the course of mobile dating app development, Tinder’s UX design was built with efficiency in mind. Users can simply open the dating application and immediately find dozens (if not hundreds) of potential matches in their area.

eHarmony: A Dating App Meant for Actually Dating

Tinder may always top dating app rankings in terms of popularity, but it is also developed a reputation as a “hookup app.” Users do not have much information about their matches due to the short length of bios. Thus, Tinder isn’t necessarily designed for finding a soulmate.

That’s why eHarmony is still among the top 10 dating apps. The mobile application version of one of the original dating websites still helps users find matches through a personality test consisting of 150 questions. Theoretically, this lets users find long-term partners instead of flings.

Match Provides Better Filtering Options

Match is a useful alternative to eHarmony because it gives users more freedom to determine how they want to find and connect with other singles. For instance, the Match dating application allows users to search for specific people, which is not an option on eHarmony.

Bumble is the Women-First Option on a Dating App List

Bumble’s interface is very similar to Tinder’s. However, there is one major difference: female users who match with males have to message them first – and must do so in 24 hours. That is why Bumble is often dubbed the “feminist option” on dating app lists.

Plenty of Fish Boasts One of the Largest User Bases

Plenty of Fish is appealing for two main reasons. First, its limited features actually makes finding matches simpler than it can be on other dating apps. Second, with upwards of 90 million registered users, Plenty of Fish is aptly-named to reflect its plethora of eligible singles.

OKCupid Makes it Clear What Its Users are Looking for From a Dating Application

OKCupid lets users include much more information in their bios than Tinder or Bumble. However, unlike Match or eHarmony, users of the dating application do not have to assume all singles on OKCupid are looking for long-term relationships. Although it can be useful in that respect, OKCupid also lets users indicate if they are simply looking for something more casual.

Hinge Leverages Mutual Facebook Friends

Hinge seems fairly similar to Tinder. The key difference between the two is that Hinge only connects users with friends of their Facebook friends. By knowing there is a mutual friend, it can help users more easily find topics to discuss when they begin contacting each other.

Happn Integrated Location Services into Its Mobile Dating App Development

At first, the concept behind Happn may not sound very appealing or deserving of a place on a dating app top 10 list. This dating application uses GPS to connect users with matches who have recently been in very close proximity to them.

Instead of matching with people who may be as far as 50 miles away, users of the dating app match with people they have likely passed on the street. This can actually help users set up dates more easily. Quite simply, users have a higher chance of finding people in their immediate area with this feature. This makes taking the next step of going on a date all the easier.

Down Promotes Explicit Honesty

Some dating app users want to find love. Others just want to hook up. Down lets those users express this clearly and without shame.

Users who are only looking for serious dates can also say so explicitly. Thus, it can be an appealing option to those who are frustrated with not being able to determine what kind of relationship a match is looking for.

Coffee Meets Bagel Puts the Pressure on With a Countdown Timer

Dating applications often connect users with too many singles to keep track of. As a result, sometimes conversations fizzle out before matches actually exchange numbers or set up dates.

Coffee Meets Bagel addresses this by curating user matches. Then, they limit the amount of time they have to chat to eight days. The added time pressure can give users more incentive to meet in person.

It is worth noting that the top 10 dating apps are not strictly limited to the ones listed here. The ones on this dating app ranking are merely a few examples of these services.
