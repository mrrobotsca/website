Stay HIPPA Compliant With Your mHealth App Development

Mobile healthcare applications have become extremely popular among both patients and physicians. They can help you consult with your doctor more conveniently; reduce the amount of time it takes to complete paperwork; and allow users to constantly monitor their own health.

The people who use mHealth apps don’t merely consider them to be useful novelties. Instead, healthcare mobile app development is already playing a legitimate role in improving the quality of care for people throughout the world.

In other words, now is the perfect time to release a product in this market. By taking advantage of the latest tech available to medical app developers, your business could develop a very successful and valuable product.

However, doing so can be challenging. App development is always a process that requires professional expertise. This is especially the case when the finished mHealth app will be used by patients, doctors, or both.

That’s because the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act, or HIPAA, protects medical records and private information of all Americans. Mobile healthcare applications tend to involve some degree of access to private medical information, and must therefore be HIPAA compliant.

Work with professional medical app developers who understand how HIPAA compliance relates to digital products.

Mobile Health App Development & HIPAA: What You Need to Know

HIPAA dictates that certain Protected Health Information, or PHI, is confidential in nature. PHI is a vague term, but it generally refers to any information about a patient’s current, past, or future health. Specifically, it tends to apply to information that could be used to identify a person.

There’s a very good chance that any mHealth app you develop will involve some degree of access to PHI. Thus, you need to ensure you’ve taken all precautions during mobile health app development to keep user data protected.

There are certain different stages and formats the data will likely go through, too. For instance, if a mobile healthcare application allows users to enter personal information about their own wellness, that data needs to be encrypted as soon as a user enters it.

Keep in mind, these types of apps often allow physicians to access and monitor patient data. Therefore, it’s very important that you take measures to prevent anyone who isn’t an approved entity from being able to access this data – even accidentally.

It’s also likely that any information a user enters won’t merely be stored on the device itself. Cloud storage is often necessary during mobile health app development in order to save space. This requires additional encryption and security measures.

Defining Your Mobile Healthcare Application Needs

Professional medical app development companies are thoroughly familiar with the legal relationship between the company that releases an mHealth app and the entities who use it. They can determine whether your organization qualifies as a “Covered Entity” (generally, a health care provider) or a “Business Associate” (an individual or organization who handles medical information on a covered entity’s behalf).

Additionally, they’ll work with you to narrow down what types of security measures you’ll need. Finally, they’ll ensure all necessary encryption and security steps have been taken during healthcare mobile app development.
