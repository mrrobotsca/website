Healthcare Mobile Technology: Expert Insight on What to Expect Soon

Rae Steinbach
2 months ago

Mobile technology has had a major impact on several different industries. This is as true in healthcare as it is in any other field. Healthcare mobile technology has facilitated easier communication between physicians and patients, allowed providers to more easily collect and manage data, and given doctors and patients the opportunity to more vigilantly monitor their condition remotely.

This trend shows no signs of slowing. Anyone who follows mHealth news understands that advancements in prescriptive analytics tools, along with other products and technology are yielding improvements in healthcare. If you’re interested in healthcare mobile technology, it’s worth paying particular attention to these key developments in the months and years to come.

The Rise of Prescriptive Analytics Tools

Healthcare providers throughout the world have shifted to a population health mindset in recent years. According to experts, population health can be defined as a “conceptual framework for thinking about why some populations are healthier than others as well as the policy development, research agenda, and resource allocation that flow from this framework.”

That’s why the latest out of the mHealth news world is that clinics and hospitals rely on Big Data to better predict their patients’ needs. However, the predictive model merely tells a provider what may happen in the future.

For example, providers can leverage data from the past to understand key trends. Perhaps patients who have sustained injuries in automotive collisions are more likely to seek care during certain times of year. Maybe certain types of patients are more likely than others to return to a hospital for additional treatment within 30 days of an appointment.

However, the predictive model is not enough. Predictive analytics tell you what might happen, but prescriptive analytics tools tell you what to do about it. If data tells a healthcare provider that patients are more likely to need treatment for a given condition during certain times of year, prescriptive tools can help clinics preemptively allocate the necessary resources to provide efficient treatment.

Healthcare Mobile Technology Leads to More Prescription Drug Apps

There are already several prescription drug apps on the market. Examples like GoodRX help patients save money. Major pharmacies like CVS offer their own app to improve upon the convenience of their services. Further, personalized delivery apps like Hims connect patients with doctors who can prescribe speciality medications. In recent mHealth news, Amazon’s acquisition of PillPack indicates the tech giant is also moving into the pharmacy business.

That said, there is still space within the mobile healthcare technology realm to create new prescription drug apps that offer genuine value to both patients and physicians.

For example, apps can remind patients to take their medication. This is helpful for patients who use several prescription drugs throughout the day. When a user confirms they’ve taken their medication, the app can relay this information to a healthcare provider.

This use of healthcare mobile technology allows a provider to monitor an individual’s adherence to a treatment plan. It can also provide useful data providers can leverage to better understand how effective a particular medication is.

As mHealth apps grow more sophisticated, they’re beginning to reach a point where they can help patients manage specific ailments.

There are many illnesses that require patients to adopt certain lifestyle changes if they want to positively impact their health. For example, someone with heart disease needs to eat well, get proper exercise, and take numerous other steps to protect their heart.

Knowing what lifestyle changes to make, and sticking to them, often involves coordinating with a physician. This can be ineffective if a doctor is too busy to stay in constant contact.

Healthcare mobile technology will address this problem. Specialized mobile apps will monitor users and allow them to provide information about their own behavior. The app can then generate personalized suggestions to improve upon the quality of their treatment plan.

These are all innovations you can expect to learn more about in the near future. Technology has always played an important role in healthcare. If trends in mHealth news continue, mobile tech in particular may have a revolutionary impact that benefits everyone from the patient to the doctor.
